# NYC Badge Set

Badges that only make sense in New York City. Written for people who live here, not people visiting.

---

## NYC City-Iconic

These are the "I've lived here 3 years and never done that" badges. Big, real, legible to any New Yorker.

**Walk Manhattan End to End**
Do the full length of Manhattan — 59th Street to the tip — in one go, on foot.

**All 5 Boroughs in a Day**
Touch all five boroughs in a single calendar day. No cheating with airports.

**Swim at a NYC Beach**
Get in the water at Rockaway, Coney Island, Orchard Beach, or any NYC beach. Actually swim.

**Watch the Sun Rise from a Rooftop**
Doesn't matter whose roof. Has to be an actual sunrise, not just early morning.

**Ride Every Subway Line End to End**
Bronze — Ride 5 lines end to end (over any amount of time).
Silver — Ride 15 lines end to end.
Gold — Every line. All of them. End to end.

**The Parks**
Bronze — Visit 5 different parks across the 5 boroughs.
Silver — Visit 12 different parks across the 5 boroughs.
Gold — Visit a park in every borough in a single day.

**Eat at a James Beard Award Winner**
Bronze — Eat at a James Beard Award winning restaurant.
Silver — Eat at 5 James Beard Award winning restaurants.
Gold — Eat at 10 James Beard Award winning restaurants.

**See Live Music That Changes Something**
See a live music set in NYC that genuinely changes how you think about music. You'll know when it happens.

**A Museum You've Never Been To**
Bronze — Visit a NYC museum you've never been to.
Silver — Visit 5 NYC museums you've never been to.
Gold — Visit every major museum in Manhattan (Met, MoMA, Guggenheim, Whitney, Frick, Natural History, Cooper Hewitt, Jewish Museum, Neue Galerie).

**The Ferry**
Bronze — Ride the Staten Island Ferry.
Silver — Ride the NYC Ferry from one borough to another.
Gold — Ride every NYC Ferry route.

**Yankee Stadium or Citi Field**
Go to a game. Not a suite, not a party — actual seats, actual game.

**The Farmer's Market**
Bronze — Buy something at the Union Square Greenmarket.
Silver — Shop only at Greenmarkets for a full week of groceries.
Gold — Find a farmer at a NYC Greenmarket and learn exactly where the food came from.

**A Diner at 2am**
Sit at a NYC diner at 2am or later. Eat something real.

**Run a Race in NYC**
Bronze — Run any official NYC road race.
Silver — Run the NYC Half Marathon.
Gold — Run the NYC Marathon.

**Governors Island**
Get to Governors Island. Walk the whole thing. Bring a picnic or don't.

**New Year's in the City**
Be in Manhattan for New Year's Eve. Not at Times Square — just in the city, doing something.

**The High Line to the End**
Walk the entire High Line in one go, from start to finish.

**Order Something in a Deli Off the Board**
Order something from a NYC deli that isn't pre-packaged and isn't listed on a menu. Just ask what's good.

## Williamsburg

**Peter Luger**
Go to Peter Luger. Order the porterhouse. Do not split it too many ways.

**Bedford Ave Full Walk**
Walk the full length of Bedford Ave from North Williamsburg to South Williamsburg in one go.

**The Domino**
Spend an afternoon at Domino Park on the waterfront. Watch the sun go down over Manhattan from there.

**Nitehawk Cinema**
See a movie at Nitehawk and order food during the film.

**Five Leaves Breakfast**
Wait however long it takes for a table at Five Leaves on a weekend morning.

---

## The West Village

**The Corner Table**
Get a table at a West Village restaurant so small that you're essentially sharing a meal with strangers.

**Find the Narrowest Street**
Find and photograph Grove Court or Commerce Street — the alley-width streets that feel like they belong in another city.

**The Bakery Run**
Hit Magnolia, Bouchon, and one more bakery on a single walk through the neighborhood.

**Late Night at the Piano Bar**
Stay until the end of a piano bar set at Marie's Crisis or a similar spot.

**Hudson River at Sunset**
Watch the sun go down over the Hudson from the West Village waterfront. Stay for the whole thing.

---

## Astoria

**Greek Food Crawl**
Eat at least 3 different Greek restaurants in a single afternoon in Astoria.

**Museum of the Moving Image**
Go to the Museum of the Moving Image. Actually look at stuff, not just the famous Jim Henson exhibit.

**Steinway Street to 31st**
Walk Steinway Street end to end. It changes completely along the way.

**Bohemian Hall**
Have a beer in the garden at Bohemian Hall & Beer Garden. One of the last great outdoor beer halls in the city.

---

## Lower East Side / Dimes Square

**Katz's**
Go to Katz's Delicatessen. Order a pastrami on rye. Do not order the turkey.

**Sunday at the Tenement Museum**
Take a tour at the Tenement Museum on Orchard Street. Actually learn something.

**Essex Market**
Walk through the Essex Market on a weekday morning before it gets crowded.

**Late Night on Ludlow**
Be on Ludlow Street after midnight on a Friday or Saturday. Walk the whole strip.

**Find the Mural**
Find a new mural you've never seen before in the LES street art corridor and find out who made it.

---

## Dumbo

**The Bridge View**
Get the photo of the Manhattan Bridge framed perfectly between the buildings on Washington Street. Wait for good light.

**Jane's Carousel**
Ride Jane's Carousel. Yes, actually get on it.

**St. Ann's Warehouse**
See a show at St. Ann's Warehouse.

**Brooklyn Bridge Walk**
Walk the Brooklyn Bridge from the Manhattan side to the Brooklyn side. Do it on foot, not a bike.

---

## Harlem

**Sylvia's on a Sunday**
Go to Sylvia's for Sunday brunch. Get there before the rush.

**The Apollo**
See a show at the Apollo Theater. Doesn't matter what it is.

**Morningside Park**
Spend an afternoon in Morningside Park. It's better than people think.

**Marcus Samuelsson's Block**
Eat at Red Rooster or any of the Marcus Samuelsson restaurants on 125th.

**The Studio Museum**
Go to the Studio Museum in Harlem. If it's closed for construction, find the annex.

---

## SoHo

**The Cast Iron District**
Walk the SoHo Cast Iron Historic District and look up — at the buildings, not your phone.

**A Gallery Opening**
Attend an actual gallery opening in SoHo on a Thursday or Friday evening.

**La Esquina**
Find the entrance to the basement at La Esquina and get a table.

**The Old Map**
Find the plaque or marker that shows where SoHo's original street grid differed from what exists now.

---

## The East Village

**McSorley's**
Go to McSorley's Old Ale House. It only serves two kinds of beer. Order both.

**Tompkins Square Park**
Spend a full afternoon in Tompkins Square Park on a weekend. Start to finish.

**St. Mark's Place**
Walk the full length of St. Mark's Place during the day, then again at night. Note the difference.

**Veselka at 3am**
Eat at Veselka after 2am. Get the pierogies.

**The Strand**
Spend at least an hour in The Strand Bookstore without a specific book in mind.

---

## Brooklyn Heights

**The Promenade at Dusk**
Walk the Brooklyn Heights Promenade as the sun sets over lower Manhattan.

**Plymouth Church**
Step inside Plymouth Church on Orange Street — one of the oldest churches in Brooklyn, with serious history.

**Montague Street End to End**
Walk Montague Street from the subway to the Promenade. Stop somewhere for coffee.

**The Historic District**
Find a block in the Brooklyn Heights Historic District that looks like it's from a different century. Photograph it.

---

## Long Island City

**MoMA PS1 on a Sunday**
Go to MoMA PS1. If it's summer, stay for Warm Up.

**Gantry Plaza**
Spend time at Gantry Plaza State Park — the restored gantries over the East River are genuinely beautiful.

**The 7 Train Ride**
Take the 7 train from Times Square to the last stop in Queens and walk back toward the water.

**Court Square Diner**
Eat at the Court Square Diner. It's the kind of place that shouldn't still exist and somehow does.
